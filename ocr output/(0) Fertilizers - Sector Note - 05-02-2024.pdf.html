Business Summary Abu Qir Fertilizers & Chemicals Industries Company is recognized as an industry leader in the manufacturing and distribution of a wide range of nitrogenous, mixed, and liquid fertilizers. Additionally, it handles a variety of other chemicals and related materials essential for its production processes, and trades in them in both domestic and global markets. The company has 5 production facilities, namely Abu Qir Plant 1, Abu Qir Plant 2, Abu Qir Plant 3, the UAN Unit, and the Plastic Factory. Each of these units contributes to the diverse production capabilities of the company. The company's products include Prilled Urea, Prilled Urea treated with Zinc Sulphate, Granular Ammonium Nitrate, Granular Ammonium Nitrate Treated with Magnesium, Granular Urea, Granular Urea treated with Ammonium Sulphate, Granular Urea treated with Magnesium Sulphate, Urea Ammonium Nitrate (UAN), and Liquid Ammonia. ABUK Operation New Gas Formula Impact: Natural gas was supplied to the company at a subsidized fixed price until the last Ministry of Petroleum decision. As of 13 September 2022, the Ministry of Petroleum decided that the price of natural gas supplied to nitrogen fertilizer producers will be calculated using a new pricing formula. This formula will be linked to the price of urea supplied to the government as well as actual export prices,

with a minimum gas price of USD4.5/MMBtu. With this new formula, gas costs rise concurrently with the price of exported urea. This exerts downward pressure on ABUK's profit margins compared to the earlier subsidized fixed price. It is important to note that gas costs account for around 60–70% of the company's production costs. In addition, the proportion of export sales also plays a role in gas pricing. As ABUK's export sales grow as a percentage of its total revenue, its cost of gas also increases proportionally. Subsidized Sales Proportion: As stipulated by the Egyptian government, fertilizer producers are required to allocate 55% of their production output to the government at a low compulsory procurement price. This quota is then distributed by the government to farmers at a subsidized price. As for the remaining 45%, the company is free to sell it at any price, whether domestically or internationally. It is worthy to mention that during the last fiscal year FY22/23, the government did not demand the full 55% quota from ABUK,

taking only 43% of its production at the compulsory price. This situation is highly likely to continue in the future, where the proportion of local sales at this low, compulsory price is below the 55% quota. Large Exports Revenue Stream: On the other hand, the company has a reliable stream of USD revenues, derived from exporting approximately 57% of its production in FY22/23. Its global footprint spans 24 countries, and it has successfully

established relationships with around 40 international customers. This would help ABUK maximize its revenues and FX gains in light of upcoming potential EGP devaluations. It is

worthy to mention that ABUK recorded around EGP4bn FX gain in FY22/23. Strong Cash Position is a Plus: Furthermore, as of 1Q23/24 ABUK boasted a robust liquidity status with a cash reserve of EGP14.5bn, complemented by EGP12bn worth of investments in Treasury Bills. This strong liquidity enables ABUK to leverage the current high-interest-rate environment, which is anticipated to rise further in 2024, thereby enhancing ABUK's profitability. It is worth noting that ABUK's treasury bills investments generated revenues of EGP1.2bn in FY22/23, contributing about 8% of the period's net profit. Future Expansions & Latest Updates Abu Qir 3 Plant Expansion: Recently, ABUK announced that it is still working on reviewing technical offers to boost the production of the Abu Qir 3 urea plant and cut down its carbon dioxide emissions. However, it is still unclear how this will exactly affect the production levels. In addition, ABUK also announced that it is currently preparing for an upcoming planned overhaul of the Abu Qir 1 plant, which is the company's oldest factory. North Abu Qir Project: ABUK holds a 45% stake in the North Abu Qir project, with the Egyptian General Petroleum

Corporation and the Egyptian Petrochemical Holding Company owning 45% and 10%, respectively. The initial announcements indicate that the project will be situated on land owned by the Egyptian General Petroleum Corporation, with the objective of producing 2,400 tons/day of ammonium nitrate, 1,200 tons/day of ammonia, and 1,830 tons/day of nitric acid. The project's estimated cost is USD1.2bn, and the company projects that it will be financed through a mix of 70% debt and 30% equity. However, in a recent development, ABUK has disclosed its intentions to transform North Abu Qir into a green project, with a focus on the production of green ammonia and ammonium nitrate. This transformation would involve using green hydrogen as the primary raw material, replacing the traditionally used natural gas. The commencement of the project's implementation will be announced upon the completion of these studies. Methanol Project: ABUK, a key player in the Methanol Project, holds a 35% stake. Egyptian Chemical Industries (Kima)

Business Summary Egyptian Chemical Industries "Kima" is a subsidiary of the Chemical Industries Holding Company. It is a key player in the nitrogen fertilizer sector, relying on power from the hydroelectric station of the Aswan Dam. Kima's product portfolio includes a diverse range of fertilizers, chemicals, and associated products, with a particular emphasis on the production of urea and ammonium nitrates. The Kima 2 factory has an annual production capacity of approximately 570k tons of urea and 435k tons of ammonia, which is primarily utilized as an intermediate product. On the other hand, the Kima 1 factory is currently undergoing maintenance, resulting in limited production output of ammonium nitrates. However, upon resumption of full operations, it is projected to yield about 300k tons annually. EGCH Operation New Gas Formula Impact: Similar to other fertilizers producers, EGCH previously received natural gas at a subsidized fixed rate until the recent decision by the Ministry of Petroleum to transition to a new pricing formula linked to export urea prices. This placed downward strain on EGCH profit margins, which were more favorable under the previous subsidized fixed price. In addition, the proportion of export sales also plays a role in gas pricing. As EGCH's export sales grow as a percentage of its total revenue, its cost of gas also increases proportionally.

Export Revenue Stream & USD-Denominated Debt: As required by the Egyptian government, EGCH is obligated to allocate 55% of its production output to the government at a low compulsory procurement price. In the previous fiscal year FY22/23, EGCH only provided 30% of its output at this low price. This situation has positioned EGCH to capitalize on export revenue streams denominated in USD. We anticipate that this trend will persist in the forthcoming periods,

supporting the company's profitability, particularly in light of the potential EGP devaluation. Additionally, this revenue stream could be utilized to service its debt, which is predominantly denominated in USD.

Future Expansion: Nitric Acid & Ammonium Nitrate Project Project Details: On 28 December 2023, EGCH revealed that it has paid the down payment on the Nitric Acid & Ammonium Nitrate project from the company's own resources. The payment represents 25% of the USD component of the total investment cost, in addition to 20% of the EGP component. The project is expected to yield an annual production of 600k tons of ammonium nitrate and to be implemented in 3 years, with a mutually agreed investment cost of USD297mn, with USD234mn to be paid in USD and the balance in EGP. Financial Strain: While the project is anticipated to enhance EGCH's value in the long term, it is expected to exert substantial strain on the company's finances throughout the implementation phase. This is primarily due to the fact that the majority of the project's costs are denominated in USD, which could intensify the existing pressure on the company's USD denominated debt. It is expected that EGCH will resort to increased borrowing as well as raising additional capital to finance this project. Recent Development: EGCH announced on 31 January 2024 that it will work with ICON India to perform technical studies and design the product development process (PDP) for

reviving its ammonium nitrate line and upgrading the final product storage facilities. This comes as part of a new project to build additional units for nitric acid and ammonium nitrate production. This move demonstrates EGCH's commitment to its expansion plans, which will increase its capacity and competitiveness in the market.
