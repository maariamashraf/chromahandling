Debt-to-Equity for Main F&B and Pharma Companies

Metrics Snapshot

30 June 2025

In this edition of the Metrics Snapshot, we analyzed the main F&B and pharma companies to assess their reliance on debt-financed assets using our primary metric, Debt-to-Equity, and its impact on profitability. To support this analysis, we chose three supporting ratios: (1) TTM Interest Coverage ratio to evaluate a company's ability to cover interest expenses through operations, (2) TTM Net Debt to EBITDA to assess a company's ability to pay off its debt using its operations, and (3) EBITDA Margin to determine each company's operational profitability. Our sample includes OLFI , EFID , JUFO , and DOMT . For the pharma sector, we chose PHAR and RMDA . We excluded ISPH as it has a different business model from PHAR and RMDA .

[FIG.1] DEBT-TO-EQUITY

In 1Q25, among the F&B sector, DOMT holds the highest Debt-to-Equity, reflecting its higher reliance on debt in financing its assets. Meanwhile, JUFO has the lowest Debt-to-Equity, showcasing its lower reliance on debt. In the pharma sector, PHAR stands out with a higher Debtto-Equity than RMDA . Notably, PHAR is the most leveraged among both samples.

[FIG.2] INTEREST COVERAGE RATIO

JUFO holds the highest TTM interest coverage ratio among both samples, while DOMT has the lowest, aligning with its position as the most leveraged in its sector. Notably, DOMT 's finance expenses surged 190% YoY in 1Q25, impacting its TTM finance expense, subsequently lowering its interest coverage ratio. In the pharma sector, we observe a lower interest coverage ratio compared to the F&B sector, with PHAR maintaining a higher TTM interest coverage ratio than RMDA .

[FIG.3] NET DEBT TO EBITDA

EFID holds the lowest net debt-to-EBITDA ratio, due to having the highest cash-to-debt ratio at 72% and the second-highest TTM EBITDA margin at 17%. Meanwhile, JUFO comes in second with a combination of the secondhighest cash-to-debt ratio at 43% and an above-average TTM EBITDA margin at 17%. However, DOMT and PHAR came with the highest net debt to EBITDA due to having the lowest cash to debt ratio at 26% and 16%, respectively.

Key Takeaways:

  • JUFO has the lowest Debt-to-Equity and the highest interest coverage ratio among both samples. However, it is trading on a relatively high TTM P/E.
  • EFID is trading on the lowest TTM P/E with the third-lowest Debt-to-Equity and the second-highest interest coverage ratio. In addition, EFID has the lowest net debt to EBITDA and an above-average EBITDA margin.
  • OLFI comes around the average in all metrics, with the highest EBITDA margin in F&B companies. It has the highest TTM ROE across both samples, with a relatively cheap TTM P/E.
  • DOMT has the weakest performance among both samples, with the third-lowest TTM ROE and trading at the highest TTM P/E among both samples.
  • RMDA and DOMT have the highest TTM P/E among both samples, with relatively low TTM ROE.
  • PHAR has the lowest TTM ROE among both samples at 18.5%.
  • In our view, EFID stands out with a moderate TTM ROE, combined with the lowest TTM P/E compared to the selected group of stocks.

[FIG.4] EBITDA MARGIN

PHAR came ahead of both samples with the highest EBITDA margin at 33%. Comes second, RMDA at 31%, followed by OLFI at 18%, being the highest in the F&B sample. On the contrary, DOMT falls behind both samples with the lowest EBITDA margin at only 12%.
