Financial Reasons

ABUK Financial Performance

Drop in FX Gains Weighs on Margins: While Abu Qir reported a 20% YoY revenue rebound in 9M24/25, both gross and net profit margins declined, mainly due to a sharp drop in FX gains. In prior periods, export-driven currency gains had significantly boosted NPM above GPM, but the recent drop in FX income has narrowed that spread, highlighting a return to core-operational profitability dynamics.

ABUK Recent Updates

Gradual Restart Following Gas Resumption: Abu Qir has been gradually restarting its production units as natural gas supply normalizes after temporary disruption linked to the Iran-Israel conflict. The restoration mitigates operational pressure and supports volume recovery for the coming quarters.

10% Stake in Phosphoric Acid Project: The Board approved a 10% equity stake in the Abu Tartour Phosphoric Acid Company, post-merger with El Wadi Phosphate. The USD643mn project, with a 3-year timeline and 250kt annual capacity, will be financed via shareholder loans. This supports ABUK's downstream expansion and long-term value creation.
