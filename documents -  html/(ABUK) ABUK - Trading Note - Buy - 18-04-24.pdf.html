The devaluation has significantly escalated production costs, a situation compounded by the concurrent surge in the free market price of urea to EGP20,000/ton. In our assessment, the adjustment in the subsidized urea price by the government would be beneficial to ABUK, considering that the company is required to supply 55% of its production at subsidized rates to the Ministry of Agriculture. Primary beneficiary of the recent EGP devaluation and interest rate hike: Considering recent economic developments, ABUK, being debt-free and holding excess cash, stands to gain from the interest rate hike, as they recorded a return on treasury bills of EGP1.2bn in FY22/23. Furthermore, the Ministry of Agriculture may not receive the full 55% quota; hence, the company has the capacity to increase exports beyond 45% of its production, thereby capitalizing on the EGP devaluation. Additionally, the company recorded an FX gain of EGP3.9bn in FY22/23.
