Event Note 8 April 2025

After the sharp drop in inflation witnessed in February 2024 on base effects, the CBE is in a position to potentially begin an easing cycle from the currently elevated policy rates. In the event that the CBE lowers interest rates, there will be varied effects on companies listed on the EGX. With most of corporate debt interest rates linked to the CBE's policy rates, the companies with higher debt loads stand to benefit through lower interest expenses.

From an overall market perspective, lower rates in the economy have a dual effect. One result is that they reduce the attractiveness of saving, including in the popular high-yield CDs (Certificates of Deposit), thereby encouraging alternative investments such as in equity markets. Another outcome is that they lower borrowing costs for companies, thereby encouraging capital investments and expansionary corporate strategies. In this report we describe the potential impacts of a reduction in the CBE's policy rates on some of the largest companies on the EGX.
